❗ Legal Penalties

❗ What Can Happen If You Hold a Counterfeit Note?

Anyone who is involved in counterfeiting currency, whether it’s banknotes or bank certificates, or knowingly uses counterfeit money, can face legal consequences. Specifically, if someone participates in any stage of the counterfeiting process, under Myanmar's Penal Code Section 489(a), the individual may face life imprisonment or up to 10 years in prison, along with possible fines.

Additionally, if someone knowingly uses counterfeit money as if it were real, according to Section 489(b) of the Penal Code, they could face life imprisonment or up to 10 years in prison, along with possible fines.

If an individual knowingly keeps or intends to use counterfeit banknotes, they can face up to 7 years in prison, as well as fines. Both penalties (imprisonment and fines) are enforceable under the law.
